MISSION & GOALS

Our mission is to launch a nationwide Movement for creating Ideal Villages which uplift and empower underserved communities through the adoption of Best Practices in Rural Development with appropriate Technology.
Our goal is to collectively impact 20% of India’s rural population through improved access to Water, Health, Education, Energy, and Livelihoods in a Sustainable way, and by leveraging technology at scale.
One of the key pillars for development in the next decade would be transforming rural communities by applying appropriate technologies to become the engines of global economic growth and create future markets.

.

BACKGROUND TO IV CONF.

Almost two-thirds of the world’s population lives in rural areas, the vast majority with poor access to the basic essentials of life, namely,clean water, access to healthcare, education, energy, and sustainable livelihoods.
While numerous non-profits, foundations and Government programs have focused on improving rural lives, these efforts were often “silo’d” or disconnected with the result that hundreds of millions still lack these basic needs. Tragically, their plight is worsening today due to the compounded effects of the Covid pandemic, major regional conflicts, supply chain disruption and inflation.
The annual Ideal Village Conference series was launched in 2016 at Stanford University to serve as a collaboration platform for diverse organizations involved in rural development to connect and evolve a holistic approach which can help improve their collective impact on rural lives.
The six Conferences since 2016 have documented a wealth of learnings and experiences from rural development efforts, covering challenges, solutions, best practice examples and project reviews, all of which may be found at: https://www.scienceforsociety.com .
Collaboration among various stakeholders, NGOs, Corporate entities, Government agencies, Universities, Civil Society, Media, and Foundations involved in rural development has been promoted through an annual Conference held at Stanford University since 2016, as well as regular webinars and NGO activities organized by the WHEELS Global Foundation.

SCOPE/OVERVIEW/BEST PRACTICES COMPETITION

  • The 2022 Ideal Village – WGF Conference will be held in India in view of the Indian Govt’s major push in rural development. India’s target GDP growth rate of 8.5% per annum will not be possible without a healthy rural economy.
  • This is not just a conventional conference but to initiate a global movement in Integrated Rural Development.
  • Given the high quality and diversity of topics, speakers, organizations and participants over the past six years, the theme for 2022 Ideal Village Conference will be “Rural Transformation for Sustainable Growth” to capture the lessons learnt and best practices from successful implementations and thereby define the future of global rural development.
  • Best Practices will be chosen in each of the 6 tracks, namely, Agriculture and Farming, Water and Sanitation, Healthcare and Nutrition, Education and Skilling, Energy and Environment, Livelihoods and Sustainability through a global competition to be completed in the next 7 months.
  • The three top best practices in each of the 6 tracks will be featured at the 2022 Conference to be held at the Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi, India.
  • Corporate sponsors will be invited to support each of the 18 Best Practice (BP) candidates under their respective CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) mandates.
  • In addition, these BP solutions will be recommended to CSR programs, State and Central Govt agencies, Foundations, for financial support and scaling across the nation. These efforts will connect Panchayat – Block – District …as the TEESRI Sarkar.
  • Criteria for Best Practice selection will include for example, Scaling potential, Implementation success, Impact metrics, Teschnology application/innovation, Collaboration levels, Stakeholder involvement, Sustainability, Unintended consequences (if any), Quality of funding, Holistic solution value and related factors.
