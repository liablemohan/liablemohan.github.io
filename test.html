WHY PARTICIPATE?

India can become a $5 Trillion economy primarily through rural development. By 2047, India must make investments to build rural infrastructure, implement social impact projects, improve agriculture, offer more goods and services to villagers, and reverse their migration patterns.
The 7th Ideal Village Conference brings together industries, academia, NGOs, and Governments to exchange ideas for scaling rural technology and raising the awareness of rural development opportunities.
Learn from distinguished speakers, panel discussions, exhibitors, poster presenters, and villagers about ‘best practices’ for implementing water conservation, scalable healthcare initiatives, emerging technologies, alternative energy, and ‘collaborative governance’ with grass roots Panchayats and Village Groups.
Meet with senior government leaders, corporate executives, and other experts in this field, to discuss various challenges for rural development.

CONFERENCE INTRODUCTION

Almost two-thirds of the world’s population lives in rural areas, the vast majority with poor access to the basic essentials of life, namely,clean water, access to healthcare, education, energy, and sustainable livelihoods.
While numerous non-profits, foundations and Government programs have focused on improving rural lives, these efforts were often “silo’d” or disconnected with the result that hundreds of millions still lack these basic needs. Tragically, their plight is worsening today due to the compounded effects of the Covid pandemic, major regional conflicts, supply chain disruption and inflation.
The annual Ideal Village Conference series was launched in 2016 at Stanford University to serve as a collaboration platform for diverse organizations involved in rural development to connect and evolve a holistic approach which can help improve their collective impact on rural lives.
The six Conferences since 2016 have documented a wealth of learnings and experiences from rural development efforts, covering challenges, solutions, best practice examples and project reviews, all of which may be found at: https://www.scienceforsociety.com .
Collaboration among various stakeholders, NGOs, Corporate entities, Government agencies, Universities, Civil Society, Media, and Foundations involved in rural development has been promoted through an annual Conference held at Stanford University since 2016, as well as regular webinars and NGO activities organized by the WHEELS Global Foundation.

SOLUTIONS MUST WORK TOGETHER

Annual Ideal Village Conferences at Stanford

The Annual Ideal Village Conferences at Stanford have created a matrix for collaboration, networking and sharing of rural development experiences, knowledge, and project learnings between these organizations.

Webinar Series

The webinar series hosted by WGF has served as forum for thought leaderships, best practices, key takeaways and practical outcomes in rural development.

Enable essential needs to uplift BILLIONS living in rural and underserved communities - for example, in India alone the rural population numbers almost a billion, more than the combined population of North America and Europe.

VISION

Enable the essential needs in Water, Health, Education, Energy and Livelihoods, in a Holistic and Sustainable manner, using cost-effective technology and stakeholder-collaboration to create an enduring rural economy for 20% of India’s rural population by 2030.

MISSION

To launch a nationwide Ideal Village Movement to uplift and empower underserved communities through the adoption of Best Practices in Rural Development with appropriate Technology.

STRATEGY

  • Identify best practices from NGOs/Foundations/Academia/SMART Village methodology for scaling across India to create Ideal Villages.
  • Receive buy-in from Central Govt. and State Govt. departments and relevant agencies to enable scaling to achieve the impact goals.
  • Integrate CSR programs and Companies that align with each Ideal Village Track (vertical) to enable expanded pilots and district-level scaling.
  • Maintain a central Ideal Village Project Office and satellite centers for all States involved in the Ideal Village scaling programs.
  • Recruit Ideal Village champions at all levels to ensure integrity, strict adherence, and consistent progress of the rural development mission.
  • Institutes of Eminence (IITs, BHU, etc) mentor 5-10 regional colleges to disseminate Ideal Village knowhow and research output for scaling.
