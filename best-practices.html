The 2022 Ideal Village and WHEELS Conference on

Rural Transformation for Sustainable Growth

Organized Jointly by Stanford Ideal Village Program, WHEELS Global Foundation (Pan IIT-Initiative) and Institute of Management Studies, Banaras Hindu University, India on November 11th-13th 2022.

DETAILS FOR PARTICIPANTS

Competition Guidelines

Eligible organizations:



NGOs, Foundations, Social Impact Start-Ups, Village Councils(Gram Panchayats selected by Ministry of Panchayati Raj), Academic Institutions, Co-op Societies/Farmer-Producer Organizations(FPOs),

Competition Tracks:

  1. Agriculture & Farming
  2. Water & Sanitation
  3. Healthcare & Nutrition
  4. Education & Skilling
  5. Energy & Environment
  6. Sustainable Livelihoods
Best Practices Evalution Criteria, will include the following(incl weighting factors as proposed below):

  1. Implementation success (vs original goals)
  2. Impact assessment (e.g. # of people affected, depth of impact); independent report if available
  3. Scalibility or scaling potential-business model, funding needed, ROIC
  4. Use of appropriate technology; innovation in approach/solution
  5. Collaboration partners, involvement of stakeholders, Govt ministers, building an ecosystem
  6. Quality of funding/support; Viability & sustainability
  7. Enabling Skills development; co-creating and local empowerment
  8. Integration with holistic solution; alignment with national priorities
  9. Feedback from recipients, end users, stakeholders
  10. Lessons learned, any unitended consequences, or side effects

Awards/Incentives




  1. Three winners in each of 6 track = 18 awards
  2. Identify 18 sponsors (corporates, foundations - see list below)
    1. Prizes (US$10K, $5K, $2.5K = $105K; INR 8L, 4L, 2L = INR 84L) + allocation of CSR funds, International recognition;
    2. Work with the winner for scaling/impact – Mentoring program (Min of Panchayati Raj..)
    3. Government support for the winners (Govt. of India, NIRD, USA, AARDO members)

      4. Min of Panchayati Raj, LB Shastri….- for the IDEAL VILLAGE MOVEMENT

  3. Incentives for all participants: Why participate?
    1. Winners will be recommended for scaling through Corporate CSR & GOI Ministry funding
    2. Free publicity and global recognition
    3. Winners can present at the 2023 IV Conf
    4. Creates awareness – attract more support
    5. Form collaborations/networking partnerships
    6. Learn about best practices from others
    7. Cash prizes to top 3 in each Track
    8. Go through a rigorous evaluation process – helps with benchmarking, improvements
    9. Potential mentoring opportunities

(Max. 2 pages, Arial Font size 11, 1-inch margin)

Purpose of LOI: The Best Practices Evaluation committee will review all LOIs submitted by entrants, and, then invite selected entries to submit a detailed Application which ensures their participation in the full Competition. Mail your completed LOI by May 25 2022 to the following: sci4society@gmail.com, AND also to: skdubey@fmsbhu.ac.in

Name of Organization:

Type of Organization: Non-profit/ Start-Up Co./Village Society/Academic/Co-op Society/Other

Registered Address:

Country:

  1. Problem Statement: The problem that you have addressed
  2. Statement of Need: The “why” of the project
  3. Your Unique Solution: eg. using technology, how you addressed this need or any other type of innovation in your project
  4. Project Activities: The “how” and “what” of your project
  5. Team, Collaboration partners: team size, location, skills, diversity, external advisors, collaborators, mentors, Govt departments; how did you involve local stakeholders in the community
  6. Outcomes: The “so what” of the project, how did it change human lives, how many people affected.
  7. Impact Assessment (if any) – how was this done
  8. Next Steps: How will you scale (expand) and sustain this project, say, over the next 5 years?
  9. Closing Statement: Give any additional information that you feel will help us invite a full proposal (eg. How did this contribute to holistic rural development; any special lessons learned? Any unexpected findings; innovative solutions, use of technology, etc)
  10. Contact details: Email, phone number(s), any other contact details for 2 persons in your organization (1 will serve as backup)

Rules:




Letters of Intent should follow the above format guidelines as prescribed, to avoid risk of rejection.

An organization can only submit only one Letter of Intent; multiple or duplicate letters from the same organization will be returned without any review.

Questions or clarification re: the competition procedure may be sent via email to: skdubey@fmsbhu.ac.in

Any attempt to influence the results by contacting organizers, judges, staff, or by any other means, will lead to rejection of your entry automatically.
